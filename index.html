About Our NGO

Our NGO was established in 2014. With the main objective of spreading the awarness for conservation of our beautiful environment.


Located between the beautiful valleys of Dehra Dun. Covered with around 2500 trees and fresh air.


Our Mission

Our mission is to foster a sustainable and harmonious relationship between humanity and the natural world. We are dedicated to preserving biodiversity, conserving natural resources, and advocating for environmentally responsible practices.

Through education, community engagement, and impactful initiatives, we strive to create a global movement that empowers individuals to take action and be stewards of the environment.

Need To Conserve Environment

There are several reasons why we need to conserve our gifted environment. Some of them have been listed below:-

1] Biodiversity Preservation:

Biodiversity refers to the variety of life on Earth, encompassing different species of plants, animals, and microorganisms. Each species plays a unique role in maintaining ecological balance and contributes to the overall health of ecosystems. Conservation efforts aim to prevent the extinction of species and protect their habitats, ensuring a diverse and resilient environment.


2] Climate Regulation:

Each species plays a unique role in maintaining ecological balance and contributes to the overall health of ecosystems. 3] Clean Air and Water:

Environmental conservation reduces pollution, ensuring that the air we breathe and the water we use are free from harmful contaminants. Practices such as reducing industrial emissions, protecting water sources, and promoting sustainable waste management contribute to cleaner air and water.


4] Resource Sustainability:

Sustainable practices involve using natural resources in a way that ensures their availability for current and future generations. This includes responsible forestry, fisheries management, and agricultural practices that prevent over-exploitation and promote resource renewal.


5] Economic Stability:

Healthy ecosystems provide essential resources for various industries, including agriculture, forestry, and fisheries. Economic stability is closely tied to environmental health, as disruptions in ecosystems can impact the availability of resources necessary for economic activities.


6] Human Health:

Environmental conservation directly affects human health by reducing exposure to pollutants, contaminants, and disease vectors. Clean air and water, along with the preservation of natural habitats, contribute to better physical and mental health for communities.


7] Natural Disaster Mitigation:

Intact ecosystems act as natural buffers against disasters like floods, hurricanes, and wildfires. Wetlands, forests, and coastal habitats can absorb excess water, reduce the impact of storms, and prevent soil erosion, mitigating the severity of natural disasters.


8] Cultural and Recreational Value:

Natural landscapes, wildlife, and ecosystems hold cultural significance for many communities around the world. Conservation efforts aim to preserve these areas, allowing people to maintain their cultural practices and providing recreational spaces for relaxation and enjoyment.


9] Pollination and Agriculture:

Many crops depend on pollinators such as bees and butterflies for reproduction. Conservation of pollinator habitats and biodiversity is critical for ensuring the availability of food resources and maintaining agricultural productivity.


10] Global Interconnectedness:

Environmental issues, such as climate change and loss of biodiversity, have global repercussions. International cooperation is essential to address shared challenges and implement effective conservation strategies on a global scale. This interconnectedness underscores the importance of collaborative efforts to protect the environment.
